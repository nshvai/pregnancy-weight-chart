About this tool

This tool was born out of frustration with pregnancy weight charts that show little more than two straight lines at different angles — a blunt instrument for something that deserves more nuance. The reference curves here reflect how weight actually changes during pregnancy: non-linearly, differently across BMI groups, and with real variation around the median.

This chart uses week-specific centile tables for six pre-pregnancy BMI groups, derived from a large international study of 218,216 pregnant women across 33 cohorts in Europe, North America, and Oceania. The reference population is predominantly of European/North American/Oceanian background — curves may be less representative for women from other ethnic backgrounds.

Bands show the P10–P90 (lighter) and P25–P75 (darker) reference intervals. The median (P50) is the solid line. Hovering over the bands shows the reference gain range at that week. Trimester boundaries appear as light grey bands. Your personal data appears as points connected by a trajectory line.

The Total weight mode shows your absolute weight. The Weight gain mode shows weight gained above your pre-pregnancy baseline — the quantity the research directly measures. Toggle between kg and lbs using the unit switcher. Enter either your last menstrual period (LMP) or your due date in Your profile — each auto-fills the other, and both together show your current gestational week. In the measurements table, entering a date in any row will also auto-fill its gestational week. Rows are automatically sorted by gestational week.

Percentile labels: below typical = below P10, within typical range = P10–P90, above typical = above P90.

Export: click the Export button to save a PNG of the chart, or use your browser's print function (Ctrl/Cmd+P) for a clean printable version.
